# Cover-Letter Drafting Spec (sample doctrine)

> Static drafting doctrine for the cover letter. Loaded into the draft system prompt before
> the learning layer (which overrides it as it accumulates). Edit it in **Settings → Drafting
> doctrine**. This is a generic, shareable example — replace it with your own voice and rules.

## 0 · The one rule
**Every sentence is specific, true, and earns its place. No claim without a scene or a number.**
If a sentence could appear in anyone's letter for any job, it is fluff — cut it or make it specific.

## 1 · Voice
- Direct and sparse. Short sentences, one idea each. Understatement over bravado.
- Mostly stripped; one line of warmth allowed in the close only.
- Active voice, concrete nouns. No em-dashes, no exclamation marks, no emoji.
- Read-aloud test: it should sound like you talking to a peer, not a LinkedIn post.

## 2 · Inputs
Research framing (angle, why-excited, honest gap, fit, emphasis), the role-fit block (matched
strengths + the most relevant gap), screening questions, and the base cover-letter backbone
(fill the [BRACKETED] slots only). **Never invent** an employer, date, metric or "why-excited"
detail — leave a visible `[ tell me … ]` placeholder instead.

## 3 · Structure (4 beats)
| Beat | Job | Budget |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | Proof or a specific company observation. Never a statement of interest. | ~30–45 w |
| **Proof** | One or two short stories: scene → number → plain action → result → relevance. | ~120–160 w |
| **Honest-gap** | Name the real gap, own it plainly, bridge to closest true experience. | ≤3 sentences |
| **Close** | One concrete next step. The one allowed line of warmth. | ~25–35 w |

## 4 · Per-beat guidance
- **Hook** — open with proof or a verifiable observation tied to this role; if it would fit another
  company unchanged, rewrite. Banned: "I'm writing to express my interest…", "excited to apply".
- **Proof** — show the mechanism (what you changed), not adjectives ("drove"). ≥1 true number.
- **Honest-gap** — one plain sentence naming it, then bridge; no over-apology, ≤3 sentences.
- **Close** — a concrete forward motion; thin warmth, no "I'd welcome the opportunity".

## 5 · Banned phrases (strip on sight)
"I'm writing to express my interest in", "excited/thrilled to apply", "proven track record",
"hit the ground running", "add value on day one", "passionate about", "I'd welcome the opportunity
to find out more", "at the earliest convenience", "Dear hiring team" (use a name or "Dear [Company] team").

## 6 · Self-check
Hook leads with proof? Every claim has a number/mechanism? Any deletable sentence removed? Gap is
the real one, owned briefly? No banned phrases? Every metric true? One page (250–400 words),
no em-dashes? Sounds like a person, not a template?
